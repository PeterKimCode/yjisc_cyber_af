Credit Recognition

Specifies the purpose and scope of school regulations and basic regulations for members.

Domestic Private Qualifications

Domestic accredited and other private qualifications can be recognized for credit after Ministry of Education approval at the President's discretion by curricularizing them.

International Private Qualifications

International accredited and other private qualifications can be recognized for credit after Ministry of Education approval at the President's discretion by curricularizing them.

Other Qualifications

Other domestic/international certifications can be recognized for credit after Ministry of Education approval at the President's discretion by curricularizing them.

Purpose of Credit Recognition System

A system granting credits by recognizing various learning experiences or qualifications outside of school as equivalent to university education.

  • To realize a lifelong learning society.
  • To provide opportunities to obtain degrees using learning history.
  • Legislation allowing degree acquisition without attending school.
100% Online Process

All admission and registration procedures are conducted via non-face-to-face systems.

Subjects for Credit Recognition

Credit recognition grants credits by clearly defining the criteria for academic performance evaluation.

  • Bachelor's Degree Examination for Self-Education system where one passes exams by studying alone.
  • Holders of National Intangible Cultural Properties.
  • Holders of national technical qualifications (Industrial Engineer, Engineer) or accredited private qualifications.
  • Successors of Important Intangible Cultural Properties.
  • Holders of qualifications completed at educational institutions evaluated and accredited by the President at their discretion.

Credit Recognition Evaluation

All learning courses are curricularized with at least 10 recorded lectures attached, submitted to school officials, and recognized for credit after deliberation and evaluation.

Chapter 4: Student Life and Discipline

Creates a healthy university culture through students' rights and obligations, student activities, and disciplinary standards.

  • Students must fulfill basic obligations such as observing academic schedules, respecting others, and information security.
  • Student council and club activities are supported according to school regulations and receive prior approval if necessary.
  • In case of violation of school regulations, measures such as warning, suspension, or expulsion are taken after deliberation by the Disciplinary Committee.
